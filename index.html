High-Fidelity Wing Design Exploration with Gradient-Based Optimization
Ph.D. Dissertation | May 2020

The design of an aircraft is determined by the demands of business aims, government regulations, and the hard facts of physics. Multidisciplinary problems like aircraft design, which can have thousands of design variables and constraints, are only tractable using a gradient-based optimizer with adjoint derivatives. To realistically optimize an aircraft wing requires solving coupled computational models of fluid dynamics and structural mechanics and then propagating sensitivities of the functions of interest (lift, drag, weight, etc.) through the models to obtain derivatives with respect to design variables. Armed with adequate and accurate information, an optimizer can explore a design space more comprehensively than has traditionally been possible by passing a design back and forth between disciplines in an engineering organization. However, the question arises whether the tools are sensitive enough to traverse the design space without getting stuck in artificial local minima. This dissertation explores the phenomena of local minima in aircraft design problems and demonstrates the remarkable capability of an optimizer to predictably traverse a large design space to arrive at an optimal solution.

The chapters of my dissertation were published in three peer-reviewed journal articles:

  • Aerostructural wing optimization of a regional jet considering mission fuel burn
    N. P. Bons, J. R. R. A. Martins, F. Odaguil, and A. P. C. Cuco | ASME Open Journal of Engineering | October 2022
  • Aerostructural design exploration of a wing in transonic flow
    N. P. Bons and J. R. R. A. Martins | Aerospace | August 2020
  • Multimodality in aerodynamic wing design optimization
    N. P. Bons, X. He, C. A. Mader, and J. R. R. A. Martins | AIAA Journal | January 2019
